Welcome! The members of the UNIMAID Taekwondo Club train in the Korean martial art of Taekwondo.

University of Maiduguri Taekwondo Dojang rules

  • When entering the dojang, execute a bow of respect to the displayed national flags.
  • execute a bow of courtesy to the instructor or any other black belt upon entering or leaving the dojang.
  • Remove shoes upon entering the dojang and place them on a provided rack.
  • Avoid bringing valuables to class, as the school is not responsible for lost or stolen articles.
  • Remove all jewelry (rings, earrings, bracelets, necklaces, watches, etc.) before getting onto the met.
  • The dobok must be clean and neat at all times. Do not decorate or sew patches, etc. onto the dobok. Remember your belt for every class.
  • Observe a high degree of personal hygiene. Toe nails and finger nails should be kept neatly trimmed.
  • Each class will have a Ceremonial opening and closing consisting of a bow to the instructor.
  • Proper respect will be given to all persons holding higher ranks.
  • Strict attention is to be given to the instructor at all times.
  • Profanity, loss of temper, horseplay, loud laughing and talking or any of lack of self-discipline will not be tolerated.
  • There will be no smoking, gum chewing, eating, drinking, lounging or lying around the met while wearing the dobok.
  • Be on time for all classes. Do not arrive too early. When you are allowed to come early, use this time to practice poomise.
  • Do not ask when you will be promoted. When the instructor decides that you are ready for a higher ahead. He will invite you to test.
  • Do not complain about strenuous conditions. Do not ask to be excused for a drink or a restroom break unless it is an absolute emergency.
  • Do not use strange terminology. Learn the Korean terminology as quickly as you can.
  • No sparring without permission from the instructor. Full gear must be worn at all times.
  • Phone the class instructor if you will be unable to attend class for an extended period of time.
  • Taekwondo techniques are not to be used outside the dojang unless circumstances involving your safety or the safety of other is at risk.
